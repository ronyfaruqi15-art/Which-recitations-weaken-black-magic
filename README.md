## 🌟 What is Ruqyah?

**Ruqyah (رُقية)** is the Islamic practice of reciting Quranic verses and authentic supplications to seek healing from spiritual and physical ailments. It is a **Sunnah** taught by Prophet Muhammad ﷺ and Angel Jibril (AS).

- **It is not magic.** It is the Word of Allah used as a means of cure.
- **It requires no intermediaries.** Allah alone is the Healer.
- **It must be free from Shirk and Bid'ah.** Only Quran and authentic Sunnah.

## ⚙️ Methods of Application

### For Self-Ruqyah
1. **Prepare:** Make Wudu, face the Qibla, sit in a clean, quiet place.
2. **Recite:** Follow the [Daily Essentials](#️-daily-essentials-the-foundation) minimum. For affliction, follow the full [Intensive Protocol](#-intensive-treatment-protocol).
3. **Blow:** After each section, blow gently into cupped hands and wipe over your body from head to feet.
4. **Water Method:** Recite into a glass of water, then drink it or use it for bathing (especially Steps 5–8).
5. **Consistency:** Continue for **7, 40, or 90 days** depending on severity.

### For Treating Others
1. Have the patient sit comfortably, facing the Qibla.
2. Place your right hand gently on their forehead or affected area.
3. Recite loudly and clearly with conviction.
4. Observe reactions: yawning, burping, crying, or trembling may indicate spiritual affliction.
5. Always begin with **Bismillah** and end with salutations upon the Prophet ﷺ.

## ⚠️ Conditions for Valid Ruqyah

### ✅ Requirements
- **Belief:** Firm conviction that **only Allah heals**; Ruqyah is merely a means.
- **Language:** Recitation in clear, comprehensible **Arabic** (Quran or authentic supplications).
- **Source:** Only Quranic verses and authentic Sunnah.
- **Intention:** Pure, seeking Allah's pleasure alone.
- **Purity:** In a state of Wudu and physical cleanliness.

### ❌ What Invalidates Ruqyah
- Wearing amulets, taweez, or charms (even with Quranic text).
- Visiting fortune-tellers, sorcerers, or "healers" who use Jinn.
- Mixing Ruqyah with Bid'ah, music, or un-Islamic practices.
- Charging money for Ruqyah (voluntary gifts are permissible).
- Using unknown words, chants, or incomprehensible languages.
